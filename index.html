Money-Saving Strategies
🏪
Speedco / Love's Truck Care — 600+ Locations
Best for PM work (oil, filters, tires, DOT inspections). Transparent flat pricing, no appointment needed. Typically 10–25% cheaper than dealer service. Use their loyalty program for extra discounts.
🔩
TA / Petro — Full Emergency Service
Full mechanical repairs at 270+ locations nationwide. TA Truck Service loyalty cards offer tiered discounts. Best for roadside emergencies — call TCA Roadside at 1-800-TA-TRUCK for priority dispatch.
♻
Remanufactured Parts — Save 30–50%
Reman starters, alternators, turbos, and injectors are OEM-quality at 40–60% of new price. Always ask: "Do you have a reman option?" Most shops won't offer unless prompted. Insist on warranty documentation.
💰
Repair Reserve Fund — $0.10–0.15/mile
Set aside per-mile into a dedicated savings account. At 24,600 mi/mo that's $2,460–$3,690/mo. Prevents one breakdown from wiping out cash flow. Keep 3 months of reserve as your target.
🔍
Independent Shops — Best Value for Major Work
For clutches, turbo rebuilds, and engine work, find a local independent diesel shop. They run 20–40% cheaper than dealers and often have faster turnaround. Ask other O/Os in your area for referrals — driver Facebook groups are excellent for this.

Common Chargeback Reasons & How to Fight Them
Late Delivery
Always get a delivery timestamp from the receiver. ELD logs, fuel receipts, and weigh station receipts can prove you were on time. Traffic or weather delays — document with screenshots.
Damaged Freight
Photograph cargo at pickup AND delivery. Note any pre-existing damage on the BOL. If receiver refuses to sign clean, note the exception. Non-recourse factoring covers broker insolvency but not cargo claims.
Short / Missing Items
Count pieces at pickup. If count is off, note it on the BOL before signing. Sealed container — write "shipper load and count." Never accept liability for something you didn't pack.
Detention / Accessorials Not Paid
Have receiver sign a detention slip after free time expires. Get the exact time in/out on the BOL. Email the broker same day with documentation — verbal agreements don't pay.

DateTypeUnitLocationInspectorResultViolationsLevel

Inspection Types & Impact
Level 1 — Full Driver/Vehicle
Most thorough. Counts fully in FMCSA BASIC scores. Violations stay on record 24 months. Challenge incorrect violations at dataqs.fmcsa.dot.gov.
Level 2 — Walk-Around
Driver and visible equipment. No under-vehicle check. Still affects BASIC scores. Usually 20–30 min at weigh stations or roadside checkpoints.
Level 3 — Driver-Only
CDL, medical card, HOS log check only. No vehicle inspection. Log these to track HOS violations separately from vehicle violations.
Annual DOT Inspection
Required every 12 months. Pass sticker on truck. Failure results in OOS order. $75–$200 at Love's/Speedco vs $150–$400 at dealer. Keep records 14 months.

What Counts as IFTA Miles
All miles driven in IFTA jurisdictions (all US states except AK/HI, plus Canadian provinces). Include bobtail miles, deadhead, and loaded miles. Do not include miles driven on a ferry.
⛽
Fuel Purchase Records
Keep every diesel receipt. IFTA requires: date, seller name, seller address, number of gallons, fuel type, and price per gallon. Bulk fuel purchased at your base — log as your base state.
💡
Credits Work in Your Favor
If you buy fuel in a high-tax state (CA, PA, WA) but drive more miles in low-tax states, you receive a net credit. IFTA credits can offset what you owe in other states — always file even if you think you owe nothing.

Q1: April 30 · Q2: July 31 · Q3: October 31 · Q4: January 31. Late penalty: $50 or 10% of net tax due (whichever is greater). File through your base state DMV or IFTA licensing authority.

About These Credit Sources
How Scores Are Calculated
FMCSA Licensing & Bond
Live check via FMCSA SAFER API. Verifies active broker authority (MC number), required $75,000 surety bond or trust fund (49 CFR 387.307), insurance on file, and any revocations. A broker operating without active authority or below $75k bond is an immediate red flag — you may not get paid.
Ansonia Credit Data
The freight industry's leading B2B credit bureau. Ansonia scores are built from actual invoice payment history reported by carriers, factors, and fuel vendors specifically in the transportation space. Unlike generic credit bureaus, Ansonia data is highly relevant — a 90+ score indicates payment within terms, 70–89 is average, below 70 is slow pay risk.
Dun & Bradstreet PAYDEX
D&B's flagship payment score (1–100). 80 = pays on time, 100 = pays early. Widely used by factors to underwrite broker receivables. A PAYDEX below 70 often causes factors to charge higher rates or decline non-recourse coverage for that broker. Check your broker's DUNS number at dnb.com.
DAT Broker Credit Score
DAT aggregates payment history from carriers using the DAT load board. Scores reflect average days to pay, dispute frequency, and compliance with load confirmation payment terms. Available to DAT subscribers. Combined with Ansonia data, this is the most freight-specific credit picture available.
Carrier411 Monitoring
Real-time monitoring service used by carriers to track broker authority changes, bond claims, and double-broker alerts. Carrier411 sends alerts when a broker's authority is revoked, their bond drops below threshold, or when fraud patterns are detected. Grades A–F. Essential for any broker you haul for regularly.
Experian Business & Equifax
General business credit scores. Less freight-specific than Ansonia or DAT but capture broader financial health: trade lines, public records (liens, judgments, bankruptcies), and bank data. Most factoring companies pull all three major bureaus when underwriting broker receivables. A bankruptcy filing or tax lien is disqualifying.
TIA Membership & Bond Registry
Transportation Intermediaries Association membership indicates the broker has agreed to TIA's code of ethics and best practices. TIA maintains a bond/trust fund registry as a backup verification. Not a credit score, but membership is a positive signal — less than 10% of licensed brokers maintain TIA membership.
Your Payment History (Internal)
CarrierEdge cross-references this broker against your own P&L and Invoice logs. If you've hauled for them before, it shows your actual paid/unpaid history, average days to payment, and any chargebacks. Your lived experience with a broker is often more predictive than any third-party score.
